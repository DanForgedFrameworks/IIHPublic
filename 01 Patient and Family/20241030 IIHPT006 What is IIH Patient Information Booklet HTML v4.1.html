    If you, or someone close to you, has just been told they have IIH, you probably have a lot of questions. This guide explains what IIH is, in plain words.

    It covers what the condition is, who it affects, how it is diagnosed, the symptoms, and how it is treated. You do not need to read it all at once. Open the topic you need — each one is short and stands on its own.

    Where to get more help and support

    You do not have to manage on your own. These organisations can help with information, your health, benefits, work and local support.

    🔗 IIH UK — information, leaflets and support 💬 IIH UK Support Group on Facebook ▶️ Watch IIH UK on YouTube 🩺 NHS 111 — non-emergency health advice 💚 Samaritans — call 116 123, free, any time 🏛️ Find your local council ⚖️ Citizens Advice — benefits and rights 🤝 Carers UK — local carer support ✉️ Email IIH UK — info@iih.org.uk
    This information is provided by IIH UK to support people affected by Idiopathic Intracranial Hypertension. It is for general education only and is not a substitute for medical advice from your own healthcare team. Information about the condition and its treatments may change over time. If you are concerned about your health, please contact your GP or specialist.
